Hello world!

Hiya, I’m Lou. Self-Proclaimed full stack JavaScript developer in the making. And now blogger too, it seems.

While I’m still working on levelling up to become a kick-ass JavaScript developer, I’m a study skills lecturer for international students at the University of York. I develop modules, coordinate teams of tutors, and do the odd bit of teaching. I’ve worked in universities in Japan and Vietnam too - similar roles, but better food.

I also worked in speech, hearing and phonetic science research for a couple of years at UCL, where I did an MPhil investigating how people understand variation in accents. I didn’t make any ground-breaking discoveries, but I did discover coding. Or rather, I was tossed into the deepens of coding without a lifeguard present. After a pretty steep learning curve with R, MATLAB and a dabble into Python, with some tears and a couple of light bulb moments, I was hooked. The power!!!

So why a full stack developer, and not a data scientist? Becoming a kick-ass data scientist was my first instinct when I decided that I wanted code for a living. But then I read lots of articles (such as this one by The Dataist and this one from Treehouse ), and saw that web development is a better fit for me. I love coding all day to build things that people can use, rather than spending most of my time on research and then using code being a tool to analyse my data. Also, the R documentation is pretty impenetrable without a CS degree, whereas JavaScript is much more accessible, so I can spend more time coding instead of translating. Win win.

Why bother writing about becoming a kick-ass JavaScript developer, seeing as there’s so many learn to code blogs already? Mostly selfish on this one - writing about my experiences will help me reflect on and consolidate what I’m learning. I can track my progress, and future me can giggle at past me’s coding efforts. It’s not all selfish though - maybe sharing my experiences can help some other people out. Saying that, I expect that mum will probably be my main reader (hi mum!).
